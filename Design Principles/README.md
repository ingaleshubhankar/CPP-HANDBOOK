# [Design Principle](#design-principle)

![Design Principle Image](https://github.com/ingaleshubhankar/CPP-HANDBOOK/blob/main/Design%20Principles/DesignPrincipleImage.png)


**Design principles** are guidelines that help us write clean, maintainable, and scalable software.
They don’t give direct solutions—but they shape how we design systems.<br>

Principles guide decisions. Patterns may emerge from them..<br>

Design principles are not a single unified set. They exist at different levels and focus on different aspects of design.<br>

## [SOLID Object-Oriented Design Principles](#solid-object-oriented-design-principles)

SOLID is a set of principles focused on class-level object-oriented design.<br>

It helps in:
 - Structuring classes and relationships
 - Managing responsibilities
 - Designing flexible and maintainable systems
<br>
Think of SOLID as the inner circle of object-oriented design.<br><br>
